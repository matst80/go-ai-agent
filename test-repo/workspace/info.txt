The sky appears blue due to a phenomenon called Rayleigh scattering. Sunlight, which looks white to our eyes, is actually composed of all colors of the rainbow. As this light passes through Earth's atmosphere, molecules and small particles scatter the shorter wavelengths of light—blue and violet—more than the longer wavelengths like red and yellow. Blue light is scattered in all directions across the sky, and since our eyes are more sensitive to blue than violet, the sky looks blue to us.
